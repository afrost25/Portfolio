About Me

Short Summary:

I am a Software Engineer at EduPoint Educational Systems with a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Arizona State University. My passion lies in reverse engineering, low-level systems, and bridging these concepts with modern web technologies. With experience in embedded systems and web development, I aim to create innovative tools that support software analysis and research.

My Journey

My passion for programming began in high school, where I taught myself Java to create Minecraft mods. With no computer science courses available at my school, I relied on books and resources from my local library. This early curiosity grew into a commitment to pursue Computer Science, beginning at Lane Community College and later transferring to Arizona State University.

Professional Experience

After graduating, I began my career as an intern at EduPoint administrators was promoted to Software Engineer I within six months. I thrive on solving complex challenges and contributing to projects that improve educational outcomes for schools across the country.

Current Focus

I am continually expanding my skills in modern web technologies and UI/UX to become a full-stack software engineer. I am contributing to reverse engineer and low-level development community by building web technology solutions that prove useful in software analysis and research.

Beyond Work

Outside of coding, I enjoy swimming, treadmill workouts, and exploring new coffee brews.
